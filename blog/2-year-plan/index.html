Mid Term Action: 1 – 2 Year Plan

Published by Trusted Cash Loans • May 29, 2025

The fires are out. Your footing is firm. This phase is about positioning your money to work for you—and setting up systems that carry your goals from idea to execution.

Step 1: Build a 3–6 Month Emergency Fund

You survived on $1K before. But now it’s time to build a full safety net. Calculate your core monthly needs and build 3–6 months of runway. This turns crisis into inconvenience.

Step 2: Reassess and Adjust Goals

Your short-term goals should’ve evolved. Are you looking to move? Change jobs? Start a side hustle? Get real, specific, and brutally honest about where you’re headed next.

Step 3: Explore Investments

Time to make your money do push-ups. Learn the basics of index funds, retirement accounts, or real estate. Start small, stay consistent, and never invest in what you don’t understand.

Step 4: Cut Lifestyle Creep

Just because you’re earning more doesn’t mean you need to spend more. Budget for fun, yes—but don’t let comfort rob you of progress.

Step 5: Strengthen Credit & Leverage It

Your credit score should now be a weapon, not a weakness. Look for strategic borrowing: auto loans, mortgage pre-approval, business credit. Let the system work for you, not trap you.
