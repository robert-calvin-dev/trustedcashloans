Generosity Killed the Cat: Setting Boundaries

Published by Trusted Cash Loans • May 29, 2025

Generosity is a virtue—until it turns into a full-time job you’re not getting paid for. If you're always bailing people out, spotting cash, or picking up emotional slack, it’s time to check your boundaries.

1. Spot the Signs of Overgiving

Do you feel resentful after helping? Are you short on your own bills while someone else eats off your dime? That’s not kindness. That’s exploitation wearing a smile.

2. Know What You Owe (and Don’t)

You don’t owe anyone your rent money. You don’t owe a loan just because someone asked. Generosity without limits isn’t generosity—it’s enabling.

3. Practice Saying No Without a TED Talk

You don’t need a 5-minute speech. "I’m not able to help right now" is complete. Let the silence sit. The ones who guilt trip you? They were never on your team.

4. Set Hard Rules and Stick to Them

No loans over $50. No bailing out repeat offenders. No giving when it costs you your peace. Write them down. Enforce them.

5. Redirect Your Energy

Want to give back? Volunteer. Share resources. Uplift people without draining yourself. Healthy giving doesn’t bankrupt the giver.

Boundaries aren’t selfish—they’re necessary.
