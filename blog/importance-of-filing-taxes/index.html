The Importance of Filing Taxes — Even If You Don’t Think You Need To

Published by Trusted Cash Loans • May 19, 2025

For many Americans living paycheck to paycheck, filing taxes can feel overwhelming or unnecessary — especially if you didn’t make much money last year. But skipping tax season can mean missing out on important refunds, benefits, and protection down the road. Whether you’re self-employed, unemployed, or earning minimum wage, filing taxes is a key part of managing your financial health.

1. You Might Be Owed a Refund

Even if you didn’t make a lot, you might still be entitled to money back from the IRS. Common reasons include:

  • Overpaid federal income tax: Many people have taxes withheld from each paycheck, even if their total income is below the taxable threshold.
  • Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC): Low-to-moderate income earners may qualify for up to $7,000 in refundable credits — but you must file to claim it.
  • Child Tax Credit: If you have dependents, this can add hundreds or even thousands back into your pocket.
Leaving that money unclaimed is like walking away from a paycheck you earned.

2. Filing Protects You from Identity Fraud

If someone uses your Social Security number to file a fraudulent return, it could take months (or years) to sort out. But if you file first — even with $0 income — the IRS has a record of your return, making it harder for criminals to impersonate you.

3. It Helps When Applying for Loans, Housing, or Benefits

Tax returns serve as proof of income. They’re often required when applying for:

  • Personal loans or payday loans
  • Renting an apartment
  • Student aid or school enrollment
  • Government assistance programs
Without a filed return, it can be harder to qualify or prove financial need.

4. It Builds Your Financial Record

Think of your tax history like a credit score for the government. Consistently filing returns, even when your income is low, shows responsibility and makes future filings simpler. It also helps if you ever need to:

  • Dispute a claim with the IRS
  • Apply for Social Security or Medicare later in life
  • Qualify for small business grants or credits

5. Filing Late Is Better Than Not Filing At All

If you missed the April deadline, don’t panic. File as soon as possible — especially if you’re due a refund. There’s no penalty for filing late if the IRS owes you money. But if you owe them, late filing + late payment fees can stack up quickly.

Need Help Filing?

There are many free or low-cost services available to help:

  • IRS Free File: For incomes under $79,000/year
  • Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA): Offers free tax prep help
  • TurboTax & H&R Block: Many offer free options depending on your income and situation

What If You Owe Taxes?

If you owe more than you can pay, don’t avoid the IRS. File anyway. You can request a payment plan, or pay in small amounts over time. The worst thing to do is ignore it.

Final Thoughts

Filing taxes might not feel urgent — especially if your income is low — but the long-term benefits are real.
