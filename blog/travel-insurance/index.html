Travel Insurance?

Published by Trusted Cash Loans • May 29, 2025

Travel insurance sounds like one of those “nice-to-have” extras—until the airline loses your luggage, your passport gets swiped in a street market, or your appendix decides to explode in a foreign country. But the truth? Most people either overpay for junk policies or skip it when they shouldn’t. Let’s cut the fluff and make it simple.

When It’s Probably Worth It

  • You’re leaving the country and your regular health insurance doesn’t follow
  • Your trip is prepaid and non-refundable
  • You’re going somewhere medically remote or politically unstable
  • You’re traveling during a volatile season (hurricanes, winter storms, etc.)
  • You have known medical issues that could flare up

When It’s Probably Not

  • Cheap domestic road trips
  • Trips booked with refundable fares and hotel cancellation windows
  • Your credit card already provides solid travel perks
  • You’re okay taking the loss in a worst-case scenario

How to Buy It Without Getting Screwed

  • Compare quotes on sites like Squaremouth or InsureMyTrip
  • Don’t buy it from the airline or cruise line—shop independently
  • Read the fine print (seriously)—know the exclusions and payout limits
  • Look for medical evacuation coverage over $100k minimum
  • If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is

Bottom line? Travel insurance isn’t a scam. But it’s not always necessary either. It’s a tool. Use it wisely, and it’ll pay off in peace of mind—not panic at the gate.
